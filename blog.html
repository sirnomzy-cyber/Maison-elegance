Building a Ten-Piece Capsule Wardrobe

By Isabelle Renard · 6 min read · Published July 2, 2026

In This Article Where to Start The Ten Pieces Building the Rotation

A capsule wardrobe isn't about owning less for its own sake — it's about owning fewer things that actually work together, so getting dressed stops being a daily negotiation.

"The goal isn't minimalism. It's precision."

Where to Start

Begin with color, not silhouette. Choose one neutral base — ivoire, camel, or noir — and build outward. Every piece you add should pair with at least three others already in the rotation.

The Ten Pieces

A tailored coat, two knitwear pieces, a silk blouse, two trousers, a structured bag, a pair of boots, a pair of loafers, and one statement piece you reach for when the occasion calls for it. That's the whole list — the discipline is in stopping there.

Building the Rotation

Rotate by layering rather than replacing. The same trouser works under a coat in January and alone in a blouse in May — the coat is what changes, not the foundation underneath it.
