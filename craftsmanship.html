How We Work

Craftsmanship as Standard

01 — Premium Materials

Chosen for Hand-Feel, Not Just Spec Sheets

Every fabric and hide is selected in person — mulberry silk from Suzhou, virgin wool from Biella, full-grain calfskin from tanneries we've worked with for over a decade. We reject roughly one in five submitted material batches.

02 — Pattern Development

Built From the Body Outward

Patterns are drafted and re-drafted across multiple fit sessions before a single production sample is cut. Where most brands finalize a pattern in two rounds, ours typically take five or six.

03 — Precision Tailoring

Cut by Hand, Machine-Assisted

Cutting is guided by hand even where machines assist, so grain lines and pattern-matching stay precise across every panel — especially visible in checked and striped fabrics.

04 — 06

Finishing, Inspection & Packaging

Hand Finishing

Collars, cuffs, and hems are finished by hand — a stage machines still can't fully replicate for drape and durability.

Quality Inspection

Every piece passes a 40-point inspection before it ever reaches packaging.

Packaging Experience

Wrapped in acid-free tissue inside a reusable garment box — designed to be part of the unboxing, not an afterthought.
